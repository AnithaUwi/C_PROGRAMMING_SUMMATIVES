WHAT ARE WE BUILDING?
─────────────────────
We're building a TRAFFIC LIGHT CONTROLLER!

Think of it like this:
- Imagine a street corner
- We have TWO traffic lights (Intersection A and Intersection B)
- Each traffic light has 3 colors: RED, YELLOW, GREEN
- We want the Arduino to turn these colors on and off
- We also add buttons so cars can ask for green light

Simple, right? Let's build it!


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 1: UNDERSTANDING THE PIECES (5 minutes)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Before we start, let me explain the PIECES we'll use:

1. ARDUINO UNO
   └─ Think of it like the BRAIN of our project
   └─ Already comes in Tinkercad

2. BREADBOARD
   └─ A board with HOLES where we plug things in
   └─ Think of it like a puzzle board

3. LEDs (Light Emitting Diodes)
   └─ These are tiny lights (Red, Yellow, Green)
   └─ They light up when electricity flows through them
   └─ They have 2 legs: LONG LEG (connects to power) and SHORT LEG (connects to ground)

4. RESISTORS
   └─ These are like "speed bumps" for electricity
   └─ They stop LED from burning out
   └─ We use 220 ohm resistors (written as 220Ω)

5. BUTTONS (Push Buttons)
   └─ Like doorbells! When you press them, they do something
   └─ These will detect when a car arrives

6. JUMPER WIRES
   └─ Colored wires that connect everything together
   └─ Think of them like the blood vessels in your body

THAT'S IT! Just 6 types of pieces.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 2: STEP-BY-STEP BUILDING (30 minutes)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 0: CREATE A NEW CIRCUIT
───────────────────────────

1. Go to https://www.tinkercad.com
2. Sign in (create free account if needed)
3. Click the big blue "CREATE" button (top left)
4. Click "New Circuit"
5. You should see a blank workspace with ARDUINO UNO already there

✓ DONE! You now have a blank circuit.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 1: ADD THE BREADBOARD
──────────────────────────

A breadboard is like the stage where all our components perform.

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Look at the RIGHT side of your screen (Component Tray)
2. At the TOP of the component tray, there's a SEARCH BOX
3. Type: "breadboard"
4. You'll see a BREADBOARD component appear
5. CLICK and DRAG it to the middle of your workspace (next to Arduino)
6. Position it so there's space between Arduino and Breadboard

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE:
```
    ┌─────────────┐
    │   ARDUINO   │         ┌──────────────────────┐
    │             │         │   BREADBOARD         │
    │  USB Port   │────────→│  (Lots of holes)     │
    │             │         │                      │
    └─────────────┘         └──────────────────────┘
```

✓ DONE! Now you have a workspace ready.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 2: CONNECT ARDUINO TO BREADBOARD (The Power Connection)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Your Arduino needs to send POWER to the breadboard.

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Find the Arduino's pin labeled "5V" (this is the power)
2. Find the Arduino's pin labeled "GND" (this is ground/negative)
3. On the breadboard, there are TWO LONG RAIL LINES:
   - RED LINE (top) = Power coming IN
   - BLUE LINE (bottom) = Ground going OUT

STEP 2A: Connect 5V to RED RAIL
──────────────────────────────
1. In component tray, search "jumper wires" or "wire"
2. Drag a RED jumper wire onto the circuit
3. One end should attach to Arduino's "5V" pin
4. Other end should attach to the RED rail on the breadboard (top line)

STEP 2B: Connect GND to BLUE RAIL
─────────────────────────────────
1. Drag a BLACK jumper wire onto the circuit
2. One end should attach to Arduino's "GND" pin
3. Other end should attach to the BLUE rail on the breadboard (bottom line)

VISUAL:
```
           5V Power ─── RED wire ─→ RED RAIL (breadboard top)
           GND Ground ─ BLACK wire → BLUE RAIL (breadboard bottom)
```

✓ DONE! Now the Arduino can send power to the breadboard.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 3: ADD 6 LEDs (The Traffic Lights!)
─────────────────────────────────────────

We need:
- 3 Red LEDs (one for each intersection? No... 3 for Intersection A)
  Actually: 1 Red + 1 Yellow + 1 Green for Intersection A
- 3 Red LEDs for Intersection B (1 Red + 1 Yellow + 1 Green)

Total = 6 LEDs

STEP 3A: Add First Red LED (Intersection A)
────────────────────────────────────────
1. In component tray, search: "LED"
2. Drag a RED LED onto the breadboard (LEFT side, upper area)
3. An LED has 2 legs:
   - LONG leg = connects to power (through resistor)
   - SHORT leg = connects to ground

STEP 3B: Position it properly
─────────────────────────────
1. Make sure the LED's LONG LEG is on the left
2. Make sure the LED's SHORT LEG is on the right
3. (If it's backwards, you can rotate it by clicking on it and pressing R)

Now repeat this process 5 MORE TIMES:
- Add 1 YELLOW LED (next to red, same row)
- Add 1 GREEN LED (next to yellow, same row)
- REPEAT for Intersection B on the RIGHT side:
  - 1 RED LED
  - 1 YELLOW LED
  - 1 GREEN LED

VISUAL AFTER THIS STEP:
```
     LEFT (Intersection A)              RIGHT (Intersection B)
     ┌─────────────────┐               ┌─────────────────┐
     │ ●Red  ●Yellow  ●Green           │ ●Red  ●Yellow  ●Green
     │  ║      ║       ║               │  ║      ║       ║
     │ ┌┴──────┴───────┴─┐             │ ┌┴──────┴───────┴─┐
     │ │ BREADBOARD      │             │ │ BREADBOARD      │
```

✓ DONE! You now have all 6 LEDs placed.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 4: ADD RESISTORS (Speed Bumps for Electricity)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each LED needs a RESISTOR to protect it.

STEP 4A: Add First Resistor (for Red LED)
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. Search component tray: "resistor"
2. Find "220 Ω" resistor (that's 220 ohms)
3. Click on it to select it
4. You may see a dialog asking to SELECT RESISTANCE - choose 220 Ω
5. Drag it onto the breadboard, placing it ABOVE the Red LED's LONG LEG

The resistor will connect:
- One end: to the Red LED's long leg area
- Other end: will go to Arduino pin (we'll do that in STEP 5)

REPEAT 5 MORE TIMES:
- Add resistor above Yellow LED's long leg
- Add resistor above Green LED's long leg
- Add resistor above Red LED (Intersection B) long leg
- Add resistor above Yellow LED (Intersection B) long leg
- Add resistor above Green LED (Intersection B) long leg

VISUAL:
```
     ┌─ [Resistor] ─┐
     │               │
  [LED Long Leg]   [to Arduino]
```

✓ DONE! All LEDs are protected with resistors.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 5: CONNECT ALL LED SHORT LEGS TO GROUND (Blue Rail)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All the SHORT LEGS of the LEDs go to the BLUE RAIL (already connected to GND).

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. For EACH of the 6 LEDs:
   - Find a hole in the BLUE RAIL (bottom of breadboard)
   - Drag a BLACK jumper wire from the LED's SHORT LEG to the BLUE RAIL

This is REPETITIVE but important. Take your time!

VISUAL:
```
     LED Short Leg ─ BLACK wire → BLUE RAIL (ground)
     LED Short Leg ─ BLACK wire → BLUE RAIL (ground)
     ... (repeat 6 times)
```

✓ DONE! All LEDs are now grounded.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 6: CONNECT RESISTORS TO ARDUINO PINS
──────────────────────────────────────────

Now the RESISTORS need to connect to the Arduino's PINS.

Remember our PIN MAP:

INTERSECTION A (LEFT side):
  Red LED resistor   → Arduino pin D2
  Yellow LED resistor → Arduino pin D3
  Green LED resistor → Arduino pin D4

INTERSECTION B (RIGHT side):
  Red LED resistor   → Arduino pin D5
  Yellow LED resistor → Arduino pin D6
  Green LED resistor → Arduino pin D7

STEP 6A: Connect Intersection A Red to D2
──────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to the resistor (the end NOT connected to the LED)
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "2" (this is D2)

STEP 6B: Connect Intersection A Yellow to D3
─────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take another YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to the Yellow LED resistor
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "3" (this is D3)

STEP 6C: Connect Intersection A Green to D4
────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take another YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to the Green LED resistor
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "4" (this is D4)

NOW REPEAT FOR INTERSECTION B (RIGHT SIDE):

STEP 6D: Connect Intersection B Red to D5
──────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to resistor above Red LED (B)
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "5" (this is D5)

STEP 6E: Connect Intersection B Yellow to D6
─────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to resistor above Yellow LED (B)
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "6" (this is D6)

STEP 6F: Connect Intersection B Green to D7
────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a YELLOW jumper wire
2. One end: attach to resistor above Green LED (B)
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "7" (this is D7)

VISUAL:
```
Arduino         Breadboard (LEFT = Intersection A)
 D2────────────→ [Resistor] → RED LED long leg
 D3────────────→ [Resistor] → YELLOW LED long leg
 D4────────────→ [Resistor] → GREEN LED long leg

Arduino         Breadboard (RIGHT = Intersection B)
 D5────────────→ [Resistor] → RED LED long leg
 D6────────────→ [Resistor] → YELLOW LED long leg
 D7────────────→ [Resistor] → GREEN LED long leg
```

✓ DONE! All LEDs are now connected to Arduino pins!


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 7: ADD BUTTONS (Vehicle Detection)
────────────────────────────────────────

Now we add the BUTTONS that detect when cars arrive.

We need 2 buttons:
- Button A (for Intersection A)
- Button B (for Intersection B)

STEP 7A: Add First Button
─────────────────────────
1. In component tray, search: "button"
2. Find "Pushbutton"
3. Drag it onto the lower MIDDLE area of the breadboard

STEP 7B: Add Second Button
──────────────────────────
1. Drag another button onto the breadboard, next to the first one

VISUAL:
```
     ┌─────────┐    ┌─────────┐
     │ Button A│    │ Button B│
     └─────────┘    └─────────┘
```

✓ DONE! You now have 2 buttons added.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 8: CONNECT BUTTONS TO ARDUINO PINS
────────────────────────────────────────

Each button needs to connect to an Arduino PIN:

Button A → Arduino pin D8
Button B → Arduino pin D9

AND each button connects to GROUND (the BLUE RAIL)

STEP 8A: Connect Button A to D8
───────────────────────────────
1. Take a BLUE jumper wire
2. One end: attach to one side of Button A
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "8" (this is D8)

STEP 8B: Connect Button A to Ground
────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a BLACK jumper wire
2. One end: attach to the OTHER side of Button A
3. Other end: attach to the BLUE RAIL (ground)

STEP 8C: Connect Button B to D9
───────────────────────────────
1. Take a BLUE jumper wire
2. One end: attach to one side of Button B
3. Other end: attach to Arduino pin labeled "9" (this is D9)

STEP 8D: Connect Button B to Ground
────────────────────────────────────
1. Take a BLACK jumper wire
2. One end: attach to the OTHER side of Button B
3. Other end: attach to the BLUE RAIL (ground)

VISUAL:
```
Arduino         Button A        Ground (Blue Rail)
 D8───────────→ [Button] ─────→ GND

Arduino         Button B        Ground (Blue Rail)
 D9───────────→ [Button] ─────→ GND
```

✓ DONE! Your circuit is now COMPLETE!


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 9: ADD THE CODE
────────────────────

Now we need to tell the Arduino WHAT TO DO.

1. Click the CODE button (top left, where it says "Blocks" or "Code")
2. A code editor will appear on the left
3. DELETE the default code
4. COPY ENTIRE CODE from: smart_traffic_controller.ino
5. PASTE it into the code editor
6. Click "Start Simulation" button

✓ DONE! Your circuit is ready to test!


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP 10: TEST IT!
─────────────────

1. Click "Start Simulation" (green play button)
2. You should see:
   - Intersection A: GREEN light on
   - Intersection B: RED light on
3. Click the "Serial Monitor" button (usually at the bottom)
4. You should see text appearing with status messages

✓ SUCCESS! Your circuit is working!
